# Impeccable — Design Context

This file is the design brief for all frontend work on the ECP marketing surface. Regenerate via `/impeccable teach`. Every design skill reads this before making visual decisions.

## Design Context

### Users

E-commerce store operators evaluating a CRO audit purchase — primarily Shopify, also Next.js, WooCommerce, OpenCart. They land **cold** from DMs, cold email, or social. They are skeptical, time-poor, and have been sold to by "CRO gurus" before. The first three seconds of the landing page decide whether they read on or bounce.

The job to be done: *"Prove to me in 60 seconds that this audit is a research report, not an opinion piece, and is worth $397–$1,497 of my money."*

### Brand Personality

Three words: **editorial, evidentiary, trustworthy.**

- **Editorial** — magazine-grade prose, literate, patient, literary. Not SaaS copy. Not growth-hack shouty. Section numerals (§ I–VII), ornaments (※ ◆ ✕), italicized emphasis used sparingly.
- **Evidentiary** — every claim traceable to a source, tier, or regulation. Numbers are real or not there. Citations are clickable. Confidence tiers (Gold / Silver / Bronze) surface on every finding.
- **Trustworthy** — operator-not-guru. No hype, no dark patterns, refund-if-the-report-fails-its-own-ethics-gate. The page itself must pass its own audit.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Pro-reference:** PicStudio.AI — dense, product-first, proof-forward. Actual product visuals above the fold. Star rating + customer count + logo wall. Real screenshots of every step. Photo-pack gallery showing actual outputs. The product is self-demonstrating; the copy *modulates* the evidence, doesn't replace it.
- **Anti-reference:** Ember-style editorial dark-mode. Beautiful typography surrounding an absence of product. Poetic copy without visual anchors. Icons replacing screenshots. "Three steps to eternal warmth" without showing what step 1 actually looks like. This is the style our current landing page drifts toward — we must pull away from it.
- **Theme:** Light (paper `#f5f1ea` / ink `#1a1410`) is the primary canvas. Dark ink is used as strategic section flips (Ethics Gate, Inside the Report, final CTA). Dark-mode site-wide is not off the table but is not the default — the brief is *operator-grade research report*, and research reports are printed on paper.
- **Typography:** Current stack is Fraunces + Libre Franklin + JetBrains Mono. All three are on impeccable's reflex-reject list. Kept for now because the voice is coherent and the user isn't asking for a re-typeset. Revisit in a dedicated typography pass if distinctiveness becomes a priority.
- **Ornament vocabulary:** § (section), ※ (pivot), ◆ (tier / emphasis), ✕ (exclusion), — (em-dash), ◂ ▸ (nav). Use sparingly; each occurrence should feel like punctuation, not decoration.

### Design Principles

Five principles that override all other design decisions on this surface:

1. **Show the report, don't describe it.** Every abstract claim past the hero needs a visual anchor — a screenshot, an annotated finding, a sample deliverable, a real citation. No more than one prose-only section per page. "Inside the Report" must contain an actual report excerpt, not CSS mock cards styled to look like one.

2. **Proof before poetry.** Density of evidence (numbers, citations, tiers, regulations) *is* the trust signal. The editorial voice modulates the evidence — it does not stand in for it. If a sentence could be written by any consultancy, rewrite it with a specific number, citation, or regulation attached.

3. **Operator-grade, not consultant-glossy.** The page looks like the cover of a research report, not a pitch deck. Fewer decorations, more primary material on surface. No gradient mesh backgrounds, no Founder-face hero, no "Featured in Forbes" logo bar unless the logos are real.

4. **Cold-open survivability.** Every screen must carry a prospect who arrived with no prior context. The hero alone must land: (a) what is this, (b) why is it different, (c) what do I get, (d) how do I start — without scrolling. No "as we discussed earlier" moments; no content that assumes prior familiarity with CRO tooling.

5. **The Ethics Gate is the product.** The anti-dark-pattern stance + three-state (BLOCK / ADJACENT / CLEAR) ethics gate is the sharpest differentiator against commodity CRO audits. It must be visible in the hero or the first scroll. Not buried in § IV where most cold readers never reach.

### Binding Rules on This Surface

- **Factual claims must match the repo.** Reference file count = 96. Clusters = 10 (per `references/cluster-routing.md`). Version = v1.1.0 (per `plugin.json`). Ethics gate regulations = 27. Baymard URL is real and safe to cite; the sample-size number is not. If a number can't be cited from the repo, it doesn't ship.
- **No dark patterns on the landing page itself.** The product refuses to recommend them; the landing page must refuse to use them. No fake countdowns, no invented scarcity, no confirmshaming decline copy, no pre-checked opt-ins on the contact form.
- **Launch pricing copy is allowed to be aspirational.** Operator bio and tone copy may be placeholder / rhetorical — "wild claims" fine where they are voice, not fact. Integrity lives on the *numbers*, not the *tone*.
